About Me

Designing Infrastructure.
Building the Future.

I am a Civil Engineering professional with a Master of Science in Civil Engineering (Construction Management) from the University of Alabama at Birmingham, graduated December 2025 with a GPA of 3.60. My academic foundation spans advanced project management, BIM workflows, construction safety, structural analysis, cost estimation, and bridge engineering.

My field experience includes serving as a Site Engineer and lead engineer among fellow L0 Engineers on a ₹1,084 Crore (~$130M) Government Rural Drinking Water Infrastructure project covering 300 villages under the Jal Jeevan Mission with Viswanath Projects Limited — where I reported directly to the Project Superintendent, coordinated daily field operations, verified contractor invoices, tracked cost variances, and maintained audit-ready project documentation. Prior to that, I served as a Graduate Site Intern Supervisor at Megha Engineering and Infrastructures Ltd. (MEIL) on the Gayatri Pump House — the world's largest irrigation pumping station, a 973 MW underground facility built 178m below surface as part of the Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project — supervising fellow interns, monitoring earthwork and structural works, and conducting QA/QC inspections on a mega infrastructure project. I also completed a structured engineering internship at Visakhapatnam Port Trust, one of India's largest major ports, gaining exposure to marine foundations, dredging operations, and multi-disciplinary infrastructure execution.

I earned my B.Tech in Civil Engineering from RGUKT IIIT Srikakulam, Andhra Pradesh. I am driven by a long-term vision to grow into a complete construction professional and eventually lead major infrastructure projects across the USA.
